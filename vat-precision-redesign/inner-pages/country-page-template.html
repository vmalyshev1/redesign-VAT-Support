VAT in the United Kingdom

The UK operates a robust VAT system administered by HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC). Since Brexit, the rules for EU businesses selling into the UK have changed significantly. Whether you're a UK business expanding abroad or a foreign seller entering the UK market, understanding your VAT obligations is essential.

Post-Brexit Changes

Since January 2021, EU businesses selling goods to UK consumers must register for UK VAT if they exceed £0 in sales (no threshold). The previous distance selling thresholds no longer apply.

UK VAT rates

Rate Percentage Applies to
Standard rate 20% Most goods and services
Reduced rate 5% Home energy, children's car seats, some health products
Zero rate 0% Food, books, children's clothing, public transport
Exempt N/A Insurance, education, health services, property

Who needs to register?

You must register for UK VAT if:

  • UK businesses: Your taxable turnover exceeds £85,000 in any 12-month period
  • EU businesses: You sell goods directly to UK consumers (no threshold applies post-Brexit)
  • Non-EU businesses: You sell goods stored in the UK or provide certain digital services
  • Online marketplaces: Special rules apply — the marketplace may be liable for VAT collection

Filing requirements

Quarterly Returns

Most businesses file VAT returns every 3 months, due 1 month and 7 days after the quarter ends.

Making Tax Digital

All VAT-registered businesses must keep digital records and file using MTD-compatible software.

Payment Deadlines

VAT owed must be paid by the filing deadline. Late payments incur interest and penalties.

Record Keeping

Maintain VAT records for at least 6 years. HMRC can request access at any time.
