General questions

VAT (Value Added Tax) is a consumption tax applied to goods and services in most countries worldwide. If you're selling across borders, you may be legally required to register, collect, and remit VAT in multiple jurisdictions. Non-compliance can result in penalties, back-taxes, and even being barred from selling in certain markets.
You typically need to register for VAT when your sales in a country exceed certain thresholds, or when you store inventory there. The rules vary by country — for example, post-Brexit EU sellers must register for UK VAT with no threshold. We offer a free assessment to determine your obligations.
We provide VAT registration and compliance services in 50+ countries, including all EU member states, the UK, Norway, Switzerland, Canada, Australia, and more. If you're selling somewhere we haven't mentioned, ask us — we likely cover it or can recommend a partner.
VAT (Value Added Tax) and GST (Goods and Services Tax) are essentially the same — consumption taxes applied at each stage of production. Sales tax, used in the US, is only applied at the final point of sale. The key difference is how they're collected and reported.
